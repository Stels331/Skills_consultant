# Layer 1: Business Model

## 1. Business Overview
**Project Name:** TOM GEAR
**Start Date:** 22.02.2024
**Core Concept:** A full-cycle wood processing complex originally designed to be energy-independent by converting its own wood waste (chips) into gas for electricity (700 kW/h) and heat generation. 
**Current Status:** The project is at Stage 8 (test launch of individual sections). The core gasification component has been postponed, resulting in the loss of the primary value proposition. A partial test launch of Section 1 is noted for 25.12.2025. [Source: TOM GEAR_input.md]

## 2. Value Proposition
*   **Target Value Proposition:** Energy-independent, zero-waste wood processing where waste generates sufficient electricity and heat for the entire production cycle, significantly reducing operational costs.
*   **Actual Value Proposition:** Standard wood processing and molding production (energy independence aspect is currently abandoned/postponed). [Source: TOM GEAR_input.md]

## 3. Key Activities & Production Capacity
The business operates across three main production sections:
1.  **Sawmill:** Processing 1500 m³ of roundwood per month (22-24 shifts).
2.  **Drying & Treatment:** Vacuum press drying, thermo-wood processing, and impregnation up to 1000 m³ per month (30 calendar days).
3.  **Finished Products (Clean Moldings):** Production of lining, clean blanks, decking, floorboards, etc., up to 500 m³ per month (22-24 shifts).
*   **Additional Options:** Small-scale mechanization for pallets, glued moldings, etc. [Source: TOM GEAR_input.md]

## 4. Key Resources
*   **Physical:** Sawmill complex, drying/impregnation chambers, molding machinery. (Gasification reactor and power distribution unit are planned but paused).
*   **Material:** Roundwood (Target: 1500 m³/month).
*   **Human:** Production staff and management board. [Source: TOM GEAR_input.md]

## 5. Key Partnerships
*   **Investors:** External investment strategy was planned but failed to attract external investors.
*   **Suppliers:** Raw material suppliers (currently facing market transformation, making it difficult to procure quality raw materials at adequate prices). [Source: TOM GEAR_input.md]

## 6. Cost Structure & Revenue Streams
*   **Costs:** Raw materials (roundwood), labor, equipment procurement and installation, energy (currently reliant on external grid due to paused gasification).
*   **Revenue:** Sales of clean moldings, thermo-wood, impregnated wood, and secondary products (pallets).
*   **GAP:** No specific financial data (CAPEX, OPEX, expected revenue, pricing strategy, or breakeven targets) is provided.

## 7. Current Business Challenges & Risks
*   **Strategic:** Loss of the core value proposition (energy independence).
*   **Financial:** Financial instability and failure of the external investment strategy.
*   **Operational:** Difficulties in acquiring quality raw materials at adequate prices; labor shortages and lack of qualified personnel.
*   **Organizational:** Internal communication breakdown, loss of trust in the implementation team, internal competition among board members, and failure to fulfill internal agreements. [Source: TOM GEAR_input.md]

## 8. Explicit GAPs
*   **GAP 1 (Customer Segments):** Target audience, B2B/B2C focus, and geographic markets for the finished wood products are not defined.
*   **GAP 2 (Financials):** Missing concrete financial models, including equipment costs, operational expenses, pricing strategy, and projected ROI/breakeven point.
*   **GAP 3 (Sales Channels):** No information on how the finished products (moldings, thermo-wood, pallets) will be distributed or sold.
*   **GAP 4 (Timeline Discrepancy):** The document mentions a test launch date of "25.12.2025" while the project started in 2024 and the current state implies ongoing immediate issues. Clarification on the actual timeline is needed.
