# Layer 1: Business Model

## 1. Value Proposition
*   **Original Core Value:** A full-cycle, zero-waste wood processing complex that achieves energy autonomy by converting its own wood waste (chips) into gas for electricity (700 kW/h) and heat generation, which is then used in the manufacturing process. `[Source: TOM GEAR_input.md]`
*   **Current State Impact:** The gasification component has been postponed/abandoned, resulting in a recognized loss of the core value proposition. `[Source: TOM GEAR_input.md]`

## 2. Key Activities
*   **Sawmilling:** Processing roundwood with a target capacity of 1500 m³ per month (22-24 shifts).
*   **Drying & Treatment:** Vacuum press drying, thermo-wood processing, and wood impregnation with a target capacity of up to 1000 m³ per month (30 calendar days).
*   **Molding Production:** Manufacturing clean moldings (lining, decking, floorboards, etc.) up to 500 m³ per month.
*   **Secondary Production (Optional):** Small-scale mechanization for pallets, glued moldings, etc.
*   **Energy Generation (Postponed):** Operating a gasification reactor and power distribution unit. `[Source: TOM GEAR_input.md]`

## 3. Key Resources
*   **Raw Materials:** Roundwood (target: 1500 m³/month). Currently facing supply chain issues due to market transformation, making it difficult to procure quality raw materials at adequate prices.
*   **Human Resources:** Labor force. Currently facing shortages and qualification issues.
*   **Physical Assets:** Sawmill complex, vacuum press dryers, molding equipment.
*   **GAP:** Specific equipment brands, facility location, and total facility size are not defined. `[Source: TOM GEAR_input.md]`

## 4. Customer Segments & Market
*   **GAP:** Target customer segments (B2B/B2C, domestic/export) are not explicitly defined in the provided case.
*   **GAP:** Market positioning and competitor analysis are missing.

## 5. Revenue Streams
*   Sales of processed wood products (clean moldings, thermo-wood, impregnated wood, pallets).
*   **GAP:** Pricing strategy, projected revenue volumes, and unit economics are not provided.

## 6. Cost Structure & Financials
*   **Known Cost Drivers:** Raw material procurement, labor, equipment purchase and installation.
*   **Investment Strategy:** Attempted to attract external investors, but the strategy failed.
*   **GAP:** Capital expenditure (CAPEX) for equipment, operational expenditure (OPEX), and the target break-even point are not quantified. `[Source: TOM GEAR_input.md]`

## 7. Current Business State & Strategic Risks
The project started on 22.02.2024 and is currently at Stage 8 (Test launch of individual sections without gasification), with a partial test launch noted for 25.12.2025. The business faces severe strategic and operational risks:
*   **Management & Governance:** Internal competition for decision-making power among board members; fulfillment of agreements is not a priority.
*   **Team & Culture:** Breakdown in internal communication, fatigue, blame-shifting, and a loss of trust/faith in the implementation team.
*   **Financial:** Financial instability and failure to secure external investment.
*   **Operational:** Inability to execute the energy-independence strategy, exposing the business to standard energy costs and waste management issues. `[Source: TOM GEAR_input.md]`
